About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the University of Calgary and a Research Fellow at the Smart Prosperity Institute. My research focuses on international trade, energy misallocation, and environmental economics. I specialize in applied econometrics and air pollution modeling.

Working Papers

The Role of Energy Efficiency in Productivity: Evidence from Canada
This project explores the misallocation of energy resources in Canada and the welfare loss it causes. It quantifies potential gains from better energy use and policy harmonization.

Uneven Gains: The Environmental Justice Impact of the RGGI
Joint with Sookti Chaudhary and Linh Pham, this paper studies the uneven health and pollution effects of cap-and-trade programs using high-resolution pollution models.
