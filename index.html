About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at the University of Calgary and a Research Fellow at the Smart Prosperity Institute. My research focuses on international trade, energy misallocation, and environmental economics. I specialize in applied econometrics and high-resolution air pollution modeling.

Working Papers

The Role of Energy Efficiency in Productivity: Evidence from Canada
This project investigates energy misallocation in the Canadian economy and estimates the welfare loss from inefficient energy use across provinces and sectors.

Uneven Gains: The Environmental Justice Impact of the RGGI
Joint with Sookti Chaudhary and Linh Pham, this paper explores the spatial distribution of pollution reductions under the RGGI program and the resulting health benefits across demographic groups.
